Devour Board

The idea of this devour board is to revolute the current cooking experience. This new concpet of a cutting board allows user to create and explore new recipes. It provides detailed process of cooking and functions as a scale. For these professional chefs, this smart cutting board provides a recording function for them to easily record the process of cooking and share it to friends.

Key Features:

  1. Create/Explore Recipes
  2. Offer ingredients subsitute options
  3. Record and Share the recipe
  4. Weigh ingredients on the cutting board

Participatory Workshop

We invited another team as participants for our workshop. We would like to gather more information from participants to validate our assumptions and concept, and get some insights from them.

We designed three activities:

  1. Participants were presented with an image of diverse appliances. They are asked to draw on the paper how these appliances will evolves in 5 years.

Goal: to see which appliance participants tend to choose and participants’ focus
Main takeways:

  • Participants focus on multifunctional devices, with the explanation that living in a tiny apartment makes it impossible (as well as expensive) to own a lot of specialized electronics.

  • The cutting board was chosen by three of the participants, adding a scale, a groove or “notch” to place the knife while cutting, and some “molds” to help aid while making dumplings or other pastries.

  1. Participants will be paired up an assigned one of the following recipes:
    Lasagna, Tacos, Falafel, Sushi. They will have to produce (something) to try to teach the other groups how to cook this recipe

    Goal: to see how participants organize information in the way that they believe others will understand.
    Main takeways:

  • Both teams indicated the necessary equipments for cooking, one of them even began the description of the recipe with this.
  • Both teams chose to draw all ingredients and equipments instead of using text. However, when showing the recipe to another team, they felt it difficult to understand.
  1. Participants will have blank cards and write down their priorities when choosing a recipe. And paste them to the wall, arrange them into categories and finally vote for the three most important ones.

Goal: to understand participant’s priorties while looking for a recipe
Main takeways:

  • Cooking time is the most important factor based on our participants.
  • Participants also hope to get ingredient easier.
