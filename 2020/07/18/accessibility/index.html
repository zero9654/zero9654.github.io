Web Accessibility - Master Thesis

The World Wide Web, also known as the Web, was essentially designed for spreading and sharing information for all people. As Tim Berners-Lee said, “The power of the Web is in its universality. Access by everyone regardless of disability is an essential aspect.” Indeed, today’s Web has significantly benefited people with disabilities. It grants them an unprecedented opportunity to access the world. However, there are still a certain number of websites remaining inaccessible to people with disabilities regardless of the growing emphasis on Web accessibility. In the context of the Web, being accessible means that the website should be designed and developed so that people with disabilities can perceive, understand, navigate, interact, and contribute to the Web.

To address the inaccessible online world pose to users with disabilities, the EU Accessibility Act and Finnish Digital Services Act was set which obliges public sectors to comply with the accessibility requirements. While the Fairdata service is offered by the Ministry of Education and produced by CSC - IT Center for Science, the official website of Fairdata is undoubtedly required to meet the accessibility guidelines. The official website provides information such as services description, user guides, training information, latest news, etc.

The objective of this study is to evaluate the general web accessibility according to the legal requirements. To assess the accessibility level of the Fairdata site, a combination of evaluation tools and manual tests is deployed to identify the potential accessibility issues. The evaluation strictly follows the web content accessibility guidelines(WCAG) 2.1, which is an international guide to web content accessibility.
